graphic design.

creating meaningful designs is my passion. i believe my works should have intent, tell a story, and hold memories. this page is an archive to store creative works that represent me as an individual.

[over] poster collection

mori poster collection

notes: the mori poster collection was created as part of design 170: visual expressions and is a visual exploration of memory, introspection, and storytelling. each piece transforms poetry and prose into layered compositions of typography, imagery, and design, reflecting the fleeting and often fragmented nature of recollection. for more information on the design and brand development process of mori, click here.

2024 wrapped

notes: following my 2023 wrapped project, which centered on the idea of monet's impressionist landscapes, i chose to theme my 2024 wrapped in media around the surreal works of rené magritte. magritte's comittment to depicting familiar objects in unfamiliar enviroments resonated with my experience this year—i was the same familiar self, yet i felt as though each moment was filled with the unexpected. as an individual who seeks to find meaning in everything, this year taught me that sometimes meaning is found in embracing the ambiguity and beauty of the unknown.

algorithmia poster collection

notes: during my algorithms class in fall 2024, our professor gave us the chance to make a piece of art based on a concept we learned in class for extra credit. from poems to songs, we were given a chance to create (so long as it remained in the realm of algorithms). so, i decided to make posters that would convey the essence of the topics we covered in this course. for me, algorithms have always been easiest to learn through visuals. for that reason, a series of minimalist posters that visually capture the core ideas of the algorithms we studied. each poster distills a concept—whether it's divide and conquer or red black trees—into a clean, striking design. i wanted each piece to resonate not just with its technical meaning, but with the beauty and elegance that algorithms can hold.

lost and found poster collection

notes: the lost and found poster collection is a series of visual works based on prose written during 2023-2024. the crumpled paper textures mimic posters one may see on the street — some of which is lost to the environment and time. each poster aims to convey the various emotions of the author, as well as the subject. i think memories are also easily lost to time, but eventually finding the precious ones are really important to me.

mementos

notes: bringing whimsicality and fun into functional designs is a goal of mine. behind the design of mementos lies a simple idea: a digital space for physical things. as a collector of various trinkets and scraps, i find joy in what physical designs can hold. starting from an initial figma design, i decided to further develop mementos into a react app, leveraging my skills in design and computer science to bring my vision to life. my react project then extended into an exploration of ui/ux design that spans across different languages, specifically english and japanese.

2023 wrapped

notes: inspired by designers and creators on twitter, as well as the viral success of spotify's wrapped campaign, i created my own "2023 wrapped in media." this project reflects on the moments that defined my year, capturing everything from personal experiences to the music that accompanied them. claude monet's impressionist works form the backdrop of my 2023 wrapped, with their dreamy landscapes echoing my thoughts on the year—nostalgic and contemplative.
